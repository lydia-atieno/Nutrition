Nutritious vegetables/fruits and their benefits.

Written by Lydia Gawala.


Vitamin A - Makes the immune system work effectively, keeps our skin healthy and helps with vision.

Vitamin C - Necessary for growth, development and repair of all body tissues.

Vitamin D - It's important for strong bones, muscles and overall health.

Vitamin K - Helps to make various proteins that are needed for blood clotting and the building of bones.

Potassium - Helps reduce fluid balance, muscle contractions and nerve signals.

Calcium- Helps to build and maintain strong bones.

Folate- Helps in periods of rapid growth such as pregnancy, infancy and adolescence.

Antioxidants - They are natural molecules that fight free radicals in our bodies.

Radicals - Unstable atoms that can damage cells, causing illness and aging.

Inflammatory condition- A situation where the immune system mistakenly attacks your body's own cells or tissues.

Satiety - A sense of fullness after eating.

Oxidative stress - An imbalance between free radicals and antioxidants in the body.

I combined some vegetables and fruits and their health benefits. However, FURTHER RESEARCH IS STILL NEEDED BEFORE ANY CONCLUSIONS CAN BE DRAWN.

Popular vegetables and their health benefits.

1. Spinach

Spinach is rich in Vitamin A, C and K, magnesium, iron and manganese.

Health benefits of consuming spinach include: Improving blood glucose sugar control in people with diabetes, lowering the risk of cancer and improving bone health.

2. Kale

Kale is rich in iron, Vitamin K, A, C, calcium.

Kale helps in digestion, cardiovascular support and promotes liver health.

3. Garlic

Garlic belongs to the genus Allium and is closely related to the onion. No matter how you use it, garlic offers significant benefits.

It regulates blood pressure and sugar, strengthens immune system, prevents heart diseases, improves bone health, lowers cholestrol, assists detoxification and it is anti-inflammatory.

4. Broccoli

Broccoli belongs to the cruciferous vegetables. It's closed related to cabbage, brussels, sprouts, kale and cauliflower.It can be eaten raw or cooked.

Broccoli is high in many nutrients including fiber, Vitamin C and K, iron and pottasium.

Broccoli is good for heart health, contains cancer protective compounds, strenghens your bones, reduces cholestrol, improves immunity and helps with weight loss.

5. Carrots

They are good for your eyesight. Carrots contain Vitamin A which helps keep your eyes healthy. Carrots are also beneficial in digestive health, diabetes control, blood pressure control, cardiovascular health and prevention of cancer.

Carrots help in stimulating hair growth, preventing skin dryness and maintaining a healthy glow.

6. Cabbage

Cabbage delivers fiber and Vitamins K and C. It's an anti-inflammatory and blood sugar regulator and contains well known cancer preventative compounds.

Many studies have suggested that increasing consumption of plant-based foods like cabbage decreases the risk of diabetes, obesity, heart disease and overall mortality.

It can also help promote a healthy complexion, increased energy and overall lower weight.

Cabbage helps to detoxify the body, keep blood pressure from getting high,

Popular fruits and their health benefits.

1. Avocado

Avocados contain an impressive amount of nutrients and are especially rich in fiber, B6, vitamin C, potassiom, vitamin E, folate and copper.

Avocados deliver a variety of health benefits. They contain potassium which works to combat high blood pressure.

Avocados contain folate. Folate is a B vitamin that is important for proper brain function and healthy pregnancies.

They are high in fiber which helps in improving digestion and preventing constipation.

Avocados may help promote a healthy body weight. Although avocados are high in calories, they are packed with nutrients and help promote satiety, thanks to their high fiber and healthy fat content.

2. Watermelon.

It's packed with antioxidants and vitamins A and C.

Watermelon helps you stay hydrated due to it's high water content. It comprises 92% water.

It's low in calories which aids in weight management.

Citrulline, an amino acid found in watermelon, may improve exercise performance and reduce muscle soreness.

Vitamins A and C, which are found in watermelon, are important for skin health.

Watermelon contains plenty of water and a small amount of fiber, both of which are necessary for digestion.

3. Mangoes

Mango is a good source of the minerals copper and folate, which are important during pregnancy, as they support healthy fetal growth and development.

Mangoes are a great source of immune-boosting vitamin C.

Vitamin C aids immunity, iron absorption, and cell growth and repair.

The orange flesh of the mango is rich in carotenoids which supports eye health.

Mangoes contain vitamin B6, which is responsible for producimg serotonin, a chemical that helps with sleep and regulates our mood.

The content of fiber, potassium and vitamins in mangoes helps keep the arteries working and reduces the risk of heart disease.

Mangoes also support hair health, as they provide a good amount of vitamin A. Substances that derive from vitamin A help provide sebum, which moisturizes the hair.

4. Apples

Apples have been linked to lower risk of heart disease. This is because they contain soluble fiber which helps lower blood cholestrol levels.

Eating apples is linked to lower risk of type 2 diabetes.

Apple skin is rich in the antioxidant quercetin, which can help regulate your immune system and reduce inflammation. Quercetin may also help protect your brain from damage caused by oxidative stress.

The antidioxidants in apples may play a role in cancer prevention.

The fiber and water content in apples can slow digestion so you feel fuller after eating. This can keep you from overeating.

Research shows that eating apples or pears regularly was associated with a 52% lower stroke risk. Eating apples can support healthy weight loss.

5. Bananas

Bananas contain fiber, potassium, folate and antioxidants such as vitamin C.

Bananas are rich in soluble fiber which helps to improve blood sugar levels.

They have relatively few calories making it a weight-loss-friendly food.

They are a great source of potassium which is vital for heart health.

The soluble fiber in bananas may help keep you full by adding bulk to your digestive system and slowing digestion.

As a great dietary of potassium, bananas could be especially beneficial when it comes to keeping your kidneys healthy. However, people with late stage kidney disease need to restrict their potassium intake.

Bananas don't just look like smiles, they promote them because they contain 6% of vitamin B9 (folate), a nutrient that may fight depression by boosting a substrate that has antidepressant properties. Hence, support a good mood.

6. Oranges

Oranges are a good source of fiber which is essential for overall health and helps keep your digestive system healthy.

They are also high in vitamin C and folate. Folate is a B vitamin that plays a role in metabolism, fetal and placental development, and many other important processes. Blood concentrations of vitamin C have been linked to a lower risk of heart diseases and cancer.

Vitamin C may also help to prevent anaemia, a condition that occurs when your body lacks adequate amounts of the mineral iron.

In summary, oranges may benefit heart health, reduce the risk of some chronic diseases, enhance iron absorption and support a healthy immune response.
